Met someone at an event or around town? Scan their personal Frequency code and they land in **My Contacts**, ready for a follow-up.

## How it works

- When you scan another member's personal code (the **Connect** code from their Zap button), Frequency saves them as a contact for you.
- The contact is **pre-filled from their public profile**: their name, and their title if they chose to share it. Their photo shows automatically because the contact is linked to their profile.
- It's stamped with **where and when you met**, the event or place if the code carried one, otherwise your city, plus the date.
- Right after the scan, you land on their contact page, so you can **set a follow-up** in one tap.

## Private to you

This is your own note that you met them. It works just like saving a business card:

- The contact is **private**. Only you can see it.
- The person you scanned is **not notified**, and they are not added to any mailing list.
- Scan the same person again and Frequency updates your existing contact instead of making a duplicate.

## Find them later

Your scanned contacts gather under the **QR Scan** tab in My Contacts. Open one to add a note, a tag, or a follow-up reminder, the same as any other contact.

## Good to know

- You need to be signed in to save a contact by scanning.
- Scanning your own code does nothing.
- Want to grow your contacts another way? You can also [scan a business card](/help/connecting/find-people) or add someone by hand.
