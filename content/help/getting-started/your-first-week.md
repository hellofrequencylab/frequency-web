Frequency doesn't make you fill out a long form before you can look around. You land in the app right away and a few gentle tips show up as you explore — never all at once, never blocking your way.

## A good first week

You don't have to do all of this, and you can do it in any order:

- **Add a name and a photo.** It helps people recognize you when you show up. You can do this from your profile or when a tip prompts you.
- **Find a circle.** Browse [Circles](/help/getting-started/join-a-circle) and join one that fits what you're into and where you are.
- **Adopt a practice.** Pick a small, recurring thing to show up for — that's the heart of Frequency. See [Practices](/help/getting-started/practices).
- **Say hello.** Post a quick intro in your circle, or react to a few posts in your feed.

## You can't get it "wrong"

Nothing here is permanent or high-stakes. Skip a tip and it won't nag you again. Join a circle and leave if it's not right. The point is to show up as yourself and find your people — everything else is just details you'll pick up along the way.
