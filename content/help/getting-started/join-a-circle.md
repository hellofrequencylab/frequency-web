A Circle is a small local group built around one Channel. Joining one is how you
go from browsing to belonging.

## Find a Circle

1. Open **Circles** from the main navigation.
2. Browse by Channel, or look for Circles near your city. In-person Circles carry
   a location marker so you can see what meets near you.
3. Open a Circle to see who is in it, what they practice, and when they gather.

A Circle with a small star is **Featured**: one the Frequency team picked as a good place to start.

## Join and introduce yourself

1. Open the Circle and choose **Join**.
2. Say hello in the Circle space. A short note about why you are here goes a long way.
3. RSVP to the next gathering so the group knows to expect you.

## Good to know

- **Virtual is the default, in person is the bonus.** Every Circle has an online
  space; many also meet in person on a standing schedule.
- **Circles stay small on purpose.** Small enough that you are missed when you do
  not show up. If a Circle is full, it will seed a new one nearby rather than add
  a waitlist.
- You can be in more than one Circle. Most people settle into one home Circle and
  visit others.
