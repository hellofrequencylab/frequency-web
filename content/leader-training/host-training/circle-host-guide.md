# Circle Host Guide

How to host a Circle that turns into real community.

## The reframe

Start here, because it changes everything else. A Circle is not a Run you finish once and close. It is a lasting local community, and the first Run is simply its front door. The Quest gives people a reason to show up and a shared thing to do, but the goal is not to finish a Quest. The goal is a community that is still together a year later, two years later, long after the first season ends.

So your job as a Host is not to run a course. It is to start a community and keep it alive. Everything below serves that.

## The model

Hosting a Circle moves through three jobs. The first two build the community. The third, the one most groups skip, is what makes it last.

1. **Gather.** Bring the right people together.
2. **Bond.** Use the first shared season to turn strangers into real friends, the kind a community is built from.
3. **Sustain.** Keep the community together and growing across seasons, through meetups, activities, and the next Quest.

Most groups do the first two and stop. Their first Run goes great, everyone feels close, and then it quietly dissolves because nobody held it together afterward. The Host who plans for Sustain from day one is the one who builds something real.

## Gather

- **Hand-pick for fit, not headcount.** The first few people set the culture of everything that follows. Invite people you would genuinely want in your life for years, not just for a season.
- **Keep it small.** Six to ten is the sweet spot, big enough to survive a few no-shows, small enough that every person matters.
- **Local first, hybrid second, virtual third.** Aim for people who can be in the same room. A video-only Circle can start things, but the goal is always real life. Pull it local as fast as you can.
- **Grow by friendship.** Friends inviting friends is how a community is meant to spread. Each season, the Circle becomes a door for a few more.

## Bond

The first season is your one shot to turn a group of strangers into people who will stick. Runs work because people go through something real together. Use that.

- **The first few weeks decide it.** This is where the habit and the friendships form, and where most groups fall apart. Pour your energy in early, welcome everyone personally, get an easy win on the board for each person right away, and ease off only once the bonds are set.
- **Get them in the same room.** Real-life time bonds people in a way no screen can. Being together, side by side, doing something, is where strangers become friends. Make the in-person part the heart of the season, not the extra.
- **Make it founding.** Name the first group founders and mean it. People who feel they are building something show up harder and bring others.
- **Small pods for real talk.** When it is time to actually open up, break into threes and fours. That is the size where people get honest.
- **Point the challenges outward.** The season's challenges face into the world, not the room. The Circle is the launchpad and the landing pad: people prep together, go do the thing out in their lives, and bring the story back.

## Sustain

This is the new part, and the most important. A season ends. The community does not have to. Sustain is the work of keeping people together once the structured part is over, and it is where lasting community is actually made.

- **Never let the rhythm go dark.** Between seasons, keep a steady beat, even a light one. A standing monthly meetup, a group thread that stays warm, anything that says we are still here. A community dies in the gaps, not in the seasons.
- **Build a life together, not just a practice.** The practice is the reason you met. The community is everything around it: meals, trips, birthdays, celebrations, helping each other move, showing up when someone is going through it. Plan general activities that have nothing to do with the Quest. That is what turns a Run into friends for life.
- **Run the next Quest together.** When a new season starts, the community runs it as a group, deeper this time, and opens the door for a few new people. Each Quest grows the community and tightens it.
- **Shift your role over time.** In the first season you are the active host, holding everything. As the community matures, you become more of a steward and a connector, letting people organize their own gatherings while you keep the thread. A community that depends entirely on you is fragile. One that has started to carry itself is real.
- **Grow leaders.** The members who light up are your future Hosts. Hand them a piece of it, let them lead a meetup, and the community grows past what one person could hold.

## The Host mindset, true throughout

You are not a teacher, a therapist, or a guru, and you do not need to be the most advanced person in the room. You are the host. Consistency and warmth are the whole job. Show up every time, glad to see people. Go first when it is time to be open. Model showing up, not having it figured out. A host who is a step ahead and honest about it beats an expert who makes everyone feel behind.

This is why a first-timer can do it. If you can gather a few friends and keep showing up, you can host a Circle. And if you have run groups before, the framing is the fresh part. You are not running another video-call group, you are building a real, local, lasting community, with a system underneath that carries the structure for you.

## A good cadence

Once a week is the heartbeat during a season. Pick a regular day and time and hold it, so it becomes a fixture people plan around. Meet in person whenever you can. If weekly in person is not possible, alternate, and try never to let two weeks pass without being in the same room. Between seasons, keep at least a monthly in-person meetup so the community never goes cold.

## Holding the room

Make it safe first. Depth only comes after safety: what is shared stays in the group, no advice unless it is asked for, and people speak from their own experience instead of fixing anyone else's. Set the tone by going first. Draw out the quiet ones by name, kindly. Watch the airtime and make room for everyone. And do not force depth, you cannot make people open up, you can only make it safe enough that they choose to.

## Taking care of yourself

You are a host, not a counselor. You are not responsible for fixing anyone's life. If someone needs more than a community of friends can give, care about them and point them toward real help. Protect your own energy too, because a community needs a host who is still here in a year. Pace yourself, grow other leaders, and lean on your Guide and the wider Frequency crew. A steady host beats a heroic one.

## Common pitfalls

- Running it like a class or a therapy group. It is neither.
- Talking too much. Ask more, say less.
- Chasing size. Tight beats big.
- Letting the rhythm drift, especially between seasons. The gaps are where communities die.
- Treating the Run as the finish line. It is the starting line.
- Keeping it on a screen. The whole point is real life.
- Carrying it all alone. Grow leaders, or burn out.

## The whole thing in one line

You are not running a one-off Run. You are starting a community and keeping it alive. Gather the right people, bond them in the first season, then sustain them for the long haul, in person, through every season and everything in between.
