# Journey Creation

How to build a Journey people actually finish. The standard for every Quest. Read it before authoring a Journey. Grounded in online-course completion research, behavior design (Fogg), and self-determination theory.

## The one thing to remember

The content is not what makes people finish. Delivery, the Circle, and the first week are. A Journey is an engagement architecture that good practices sit inside. Build the architecture first.

## The five rules

1. **Push, do not make them pull.** One clear thing arrives each day. Never build a library people have to remember to open. Self-paced courses finish under 15 percent; daily-drip challenges finish at 70 to 80 percent.
2. **The Circle carries it.** People finish in a cohort and quit alone. Cohort programs finish above 85 percent. Every Journey is built to be run by a Circle. Solo is an on-ramp, nudged toward a Circle fast.
3. **Hit the three needs.** People keep going when a Journey gives them autonomy (real choice), competence (visible progress), and relatedness (being seen). Points can fake choice and belonging. They cannot fake competence, so the practices must produce progress a person can feel.
4. **Win the first week.** Most people who quit, quit early. A Journey has to pay out a real win in its first days, or the rest never happens. Design the first three days to be unmissable.
5. **The game is scaffolding, not the point.** Use Zaps and streaks to carry people to the real outcome, then get out of the way. Over-gamifying a Journey hollows it out.

## The four pillars

- **Mind is mental health.** A clear, settled head.
- **Body is physical health.** Movement, energy, sleep, the basics.
- **Spirit is emotions and personal energy.** How you feel and how charged you are.
- **Expression is the output.** What you put into the world when the other three are in balance. Not a fourth kind of practice, the result of the first three.

Every Quest runs three Journeys, one each for Mind, Body, and Spirit. Each one ends in an Expression Challenge, which is the proof that the balance took.

## Practices are open

The library is a seed, not a cage. People create their own practices and tag them by pillar and however else they want. The point is a life of intentional, integrated practices that feel natural and easy, not a fixed checklist someone hands you. The practices in the library are the starter pack, not the whole set. A practice carries an owner and a tag set, so anyone can add their own and file it where it belongs.

## Anatomy of a Journey

- **Premise.** One line: the shift this Journey creates. Lead with the problem, not the practice.
- **Practices.** Five, each tagged Light, Standard, or Heavy. Each has a five-minute floor so it can be done on a bad day. Anchor each to an existing daily routine. Pull from the library or let members bring their own.
- **The daily loop.** A contextual nudge, a tiny action, an instant Zap so it counts the moment it is logged, and visible progress toward the next Trophy.
- **The capstone (Challenge).** One fun act that pushes the member out of their comfort zone through expression. It is the proof of the Journey and the most human part of it. Do not cut it.

## How to build one

1. Name the shift in one line. If it sounds like a promise of transformation, rewrite it as a problem.
2. Pick the pillar: Mind, Body, or Spirit.
3. Choose five practices. Range across effort, some Light, some Heavy. Each gets a five-minute version. Library or member-made.
4. Anchor each practice to a real moment in a normal day.
5. Make the first three days unmissable. The first win comes fast.
6. Write the capstone: one fun, slightly uncomfortable act of expression, done with or in front of people, with a solo fallback.
7. Name it plainly. One accent word. No kitsch, no ancient-wisdom dressing.
8. Check it against the five rules before shipping.

## Fixed mechanics (do not reinvent)

- Log a practice: Light 8, Standard 12, Heavy 15 Zaps.
- Finish a Journey: log on 14 to 16 of its 28 days and do the capstone. Pays +75 Zaps, a Trophy, and Gems (25, 50, 100 for the first, second, third Journey of the season).
- Capstone: +50 Zaps in person at a Circle, or +30 Gems solo.
- Three finished Journeys in a season is Master.

## Voice

Plain sentences. Proper nouns carry the weight, the rest stays ordinary. No em dashes. One accent word per name. Specific sensory outcomes and honest time asks. Never narrate the member's feelings for them.
