Circles are meant to stay small. Small is where people are missed when they do not
show up, where everyone is known, where trust forms. So when a circle fills, the goal
is not to get bigger. It is to seed another circle.

## Why splitting beats growing

A group of eight where everyone matters beats a group of thirty where most people
hide. When you split, you do not lose people, you multiply belonging and you grow a
new leader. This is how a few neighboring circles become a whole local community,
bottom-up, with no one appointed from above.

## When to split

Watch for the signs:

- Gatherings feel crowded, and quieter people stop speaking up.
- You no longer remember everyone's name or story.
- Two natural clusters are forming on their own.

That is the moment.

## How to split well

1. **Spot the next host.** Someone already showing up, helping, and ready to step up.
   Leaders rise from showing up, not from being appointed.
2. **Bless it openly.** Frame the new circle as growth, not loss. Celebrate it.
3. **Seed, do not scatter.** Help the new host carry a handful of members and a clear
   time and place, so it begins as a real circle, not a fragment.
4. **Stay connected.** The two circles share a neighborhood. Gather together
   occasionally; the bond remains.
