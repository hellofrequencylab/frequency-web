Every circle has slow stretches: a holiday, a hard season, a few thin gatherings. A
circle does not die from a quiet week. It fades when no one tends it through one. The
good news is that tending is small and steady, not heroic.

## The habits that keep it alive

- **Protect the standing time.** A reliable rhythm is the heartbeat. Keep it even when
  numbers dip; consistency is what people quietly count on.
- **Reach out between gatherings.** A short message to someone you have not seen in a
  while does more than any announcement. People return when they feel missed.
- **Keep it small and shared.** Hand off pieces to others so the circle does not rest
  on you alone. Shared ownership is what survives any one person stepping back.
- **Mark the practice.** Logging what you do, together, makes the invisible progress
  visible, and visible progress keeps people coming.

## When it is genuinely struggling

- Talk to the people who still show up. Ask what would make it worth their week.
- Shrink before you quit. A smaller, truer circle is better than a big tired one.
- Consider a merge with a neighboring circle for a season, then re-seed later.

A circle is not a project to finish. It is a small, living thing you tend. Tend it
lightly and steadily, and it will outlast any single rough patch.
