You do not need to be an expert, a teacher, or a leader to start a circle. You just
need to care about something and be willing to invite a few people to share it. This
is the whole job.

## 1. Pick what you practice

Choose one thing you genuinely do, or want to do regularly: morning movement, a book
discussion, breathwork, a walk, a meal, a skill. Specific beats grand. "Tuesday
evening run, then tea" is a better circle than "wellness."

## 2. Name it and claim a place

Create your circle, give it a clear name, set your Interest, and pick where and when
you will meet. Even a rough time and a public spot is enough to begin. A standing time
is the single biggest thing that makes a circle real.

## 3. Invite five people

Message five people directly. Not a broadcast, five real invitations. Tell them what
it is, when, and that you would love to see them there. Three saying yes is a circle.

## 4. Show up, even if it is small

The first gathering might be two of you. That is normal and it counts. Showing up when
it is small is exactly how circles take root. Log your practice, mark the gathering,
and invite the next person.

That is it. You have started a circle. Next, learn how to
[run a great gathering](/programs/run-a-great-gathering).
