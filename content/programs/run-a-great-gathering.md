A great gathering is not about being impressive. It is about people leaving feeling
more connected than when they arrived. A simple, repeatable rhythm does that better
than anything elaborate.

## A rhythm that works

- **Open with a welcome.** Greet each person by name. A warm start sets everything.
- **Do the thing.** Spend most of the time on the practice itself, not on logistics.
- **Make space to connect.** A few minutes where people actually talk to each other,
  not just to you. A single question round works wonders.
- **Close with what's next.** Name the next gathering before anyone leaves, so showing
  up again is the default.

## A few quiet habits of good hosts

- Start on time, even with a few people. It honors the ones who came.
- Notice the newcomer and the quiet one. A small bit of attention keeps them coming.
- Share the load. Invite others to lead a part. Leaderful, not leader-dependent.
- Follow up afterward with anyone who missed it. "We missed you" brings people back.

## Keep it sustainable

You do not have to carry it alone, and you should not try to. The point is the
practice, the place, and the people, not your performance. Keep it light enough that
you look forward to it too.
